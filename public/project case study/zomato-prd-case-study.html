01Problem statement

Customers — typically office employees in tier 1 cities with high order frequency — forget to leave a review once an order is delivered.

Nudging them will raise user-generated content, customer trust and retention, ultimately boosting average orders and revenue. It also helps users make better decisions on future orders, creating a content loop for growth.

Company reports show a decline in the YoY growth rate of average monthly transacting users — a strong signal that engagement, not acquisition, is the lever to pull.

02Goals & non-goals

Goals

Goal 01
Increase % of reviewers (reviewers ÷ total users)
Goal 02
Increase reviews submitted per reviewer
Goal 03
Increase review completion rate (reduce churn on the form)
Goal 04
Decrease time between delivery and review submission

Non-goals

  • Not aimed at increasing the number of orders in order to grow reviews.
  • Not committing to improve quality of reviews or measure customer satisfaction through them.
  • Limited to English text reviews — multilingual support is out of scope.

06Proposed solutions

In priority order (mind-map & prioritisation matrix in appendix):

1

Push notifications & email reminders Top priority

Send timely reminders through existing push and email infrastructure within a 24-hour target window after delivery — leveraging channels users already check.

2

In-app prompts

Whenever the user opens the app, prompt them about reviewing the last order through pop-ups and contextually placed banners.

3

Review prompts in receipts

Add a review CTA inside the invoice and delivery receipt — artefacts users naturally open to verify their order post delivery.

4

Streak points & leaderboard

Gamify reviewing: award points per review, reward streaks and surface a public leaderboard for community-driven engagement.

07Key features

  • Reminders via push notifications and email after a delay from delivery.
  • In-app prompts nudge users about pending reviews while they explore the app.
  • Submit reviews directly from email invoices, with a deep-link redirect to the review page.
  • Streak score & leaderboard, with offers for the top reviewers.

09Logical & system design

Algorithms

  • Order completion timer: when an order is marked delivered, schedule a reminder notification after a set time (e.g. 30 minutes).
  • Notification throttling: cap how many review requests a user can receive in a window — critical for high-frequency orderers.

Schema changes

Order table
Column tracking whether a reminder notification was sent.
Notification table
Stores sent notifications: timestamp, channel, type.
User activity table
Tracks if review prompt was shown and whether the user interacted.
Reminder scheduler
Event-trigger system queues post-delivery reminder jobs.

System design (high level)

Order completion → event-trigger system → reminder scheduler → message queue → workers fan out to FCM (Android), APNs (iOS), SMS provider and SMTP email. A notification server reads templates and logs from cache + database; an analytics service tracks click and send status with retry-on-error.

10Technical considerations

Scalability
Handle high reminder volumes & user actions during peak hours.
Fault tolerance
Retry mechanism for failed sends; graceful failure handling.
Latency
Low latency for reminders & user interactions is critical.
Security & compliance
Data handled per GDPR & CCPA; transparent opt-in.

Challenges

  • Notification fatigue: dynamic scheduling and user-controlled preferences.
  • User privacy: opt-in mechanisms and transparency around data usage.
  • Personalisation at scale: templating engines to generate dynamic content efficiently.

Long term
Gamification, social sharing, deeper restaurant collaboration.
Open questions
Ideal cadence? Best channels? How to re-engage opt-outs?
Trade-off considered. Pushing for immediate reviews may boost short-term volume but feel transactional, eroding long-term loyalty. Frequent reminders also load the back-end at peak.
