01 — Overview

The problem nobody plans for

When someone passes away, their digital life doesn't. Bank logins, crypto wallets, cloud-stored photos, recurring subscriptions, social accounts, and personal letters remain locked behind passwords nobody else knows.

Aeterna is a trust-first platform that securely stores digital assets, verifies a person's wellbeing through a multi-signal Dead Man's Switch, and transfers access to chosen heirs through a guided AI executor — without the platform itself ever seeing the contents.

02 — Problem

Why this matters now

Digital wealth and digital memory have outpaced inheritance law and consumer tooling. Existing solutions are either legal-form generators or password managers — neither solves the actual handover problem.

Market signal

70%+ of adults have digital assets they consider valuable. Less than 10% have any transfer plan. Crypto custody alone has an estimated $140B+ in lost wealth from missing keys. The category is real; the product experience is missing.

03 — Users

Who we built for

The Grantor

35–60, financially organized, has crypto, cloud assets and dependents. Wants peace of mind without paranoia.

Set it up once, forget it, trust it.

The Heir

Adult child or partner. Grieving, overwhelmed, non-technical. Needs to be walked through every step.

Understand what was left and what to do next.

The Keeper

Trusted friend, sibling, or lawyer. Holds 1 of 3 cryptographic shards. Doesn't see vault contents.

Confirm or reject activation only when truly needed.

04 — Product Strategy

From vault to executor

Most competitors stop at storage. Our insight: storage is table-stakes — the real value is the handover moment. We re-framed the product around three strategic bets.

Bet 1 — Trust as the product

Brand, copy and UI styled like a private bank or memorial. Slow fades, serif headings, deep ink + warm cream. Zero-knowledge architecture mentioned explicitly on every trust-bearing surface.

Bet 2 — Multi-signal Dead Man's Switch

Single-signal switches false-trigger constantly. We combined manual pulse, login activity, and Keeper verification with graduated escalation to make false-positives nearly impossible.

Bet 3 — Aeterna AI as a guided executor

Not a chatbot. A directive assistant that tracks a 4-step setup flow for the grantor, and walks the heir through inheritance tasks post-activation.

05 — The Solution

How Aeterna works

  1. 01VaultEncrypt accounts, credentials, documents, letters and memories — client-side, before upload.
  2. 02HeirsInvite beneficiaries with per-item permissions and release conditions.
  3. 03KeepersDistribute Shamir 2-of-3 shards to three trusted people. They never see vault contents.
  4. 04PulseConfigure check-in cadence. Healthy pulses = silence. Missed pulses = graduated escalation.
  5. 05Activation2-of-3 Keepers verify status → master key reconstructs → heirs onboarded by AI executor.
Aeterna AI — the differentiator

Re-designed from a generic chatbot into a guided executor. It tracks the user's real setup state (assets added, heirs invited, keepers sealed, pulse configured) and produces brief, directive next-step actions with one-click navigation. After activation, the same assistant becomes the heir's empathetic guide through the inheritance checklist.

07 — Architecture & Security

Zero-knowledge by construction

The server stores ciphertext only. Vault items are encrypted in the browser using AES-GCM with a key derived from the user's passphrase via PBKDF2. The master key is split into three Shamir shards distributed to Keepers — any 2 can reconstruct it; no single party (including Aeterna) ever holds it whole.

Switch state machine

Healthy → Missed Pulse → Grace 1 (gentle reminder, +3d) → Grace 2 (urgent + backup contact, +7d) → Grace 3 (notify Keepers, +14d) → Keeper verification (2-of-3) → Activation → Heir onboarding. Any successful pulse, login, or single Keeper veto resets the state.

08 — Key PM Decisions

Trade-offs and why

Client-side encryption vs. server convenience

Chose client-side. Lost: server-side search, easy passphrase recovery. Gained: a zero-knowledge promise we can defend in marketing and law.

Shamir 2-of-3 vs. single-recovery key

Chose Shamir. Lost: simpler onboarding. Gained: no single point of failure, no Aeterna-as-custodian liability.

Aeterna AI as executor vs. open chatbot

Chose directive executor. Lost: 'wow, it's ChatGPT'. Gained: measurable setup-completion lift and zero hallucinated promises.

Multi-signal switch vs. single pulse

Chose multi-signal. Lost: simpler logic. Gained: 0 false activations across 3-month internal simulation.

Web-first vs. mobile-first

Chose web. Setup is a once-a-quarter laptop task, not a daily mobile habit. Pulse check-in works via signed email/SMS link.

12 — Learnings

What this project taught me

Trust is a UX problem, not a copy problem.

Adding 'AES-256 encrypted' to a page does nothing. Slowing animations, removing exclamation marks, and choosing a serif heading did more for perceived security than any badge.

AI is most valuable when it stops being a chatbot.

The Aeterna AI rebuild — from open chat to directive executor — was the single biggest setup-completion win. Constraint creates clarity.

Sensitive products need rehearsal, not onboarding.

We added a 'simulate time passing' control so users can rehearse their own activation. It became the most-shared feature in user interviews.

Zero-knowledge forces honesty.

Because we genuinely cannot recover a lost passphrase, every flow had to be designed assuming the user would forget. That constraint produced a better recovery model than any feature spec would have.
